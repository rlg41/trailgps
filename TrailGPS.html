Device orientation comes from the iPad's internal magnetometer. It can be accurate once calibrated, but may be thrown off by nearby metal or need calibration — if the heading looks wrong, wave the iPad in a figure-8 a few times. An accuracy figure appears next to the reading when available.
GPS travel heading is derived from movement, so it is meaningful only while you are walking. It becomes unreliable when you stop.
Device compass calibration
If the device heading reads consistently off, adjust the offset until it matches a known direction. Use the −/+ buttons for fine steps, or tap the number to type a value.
